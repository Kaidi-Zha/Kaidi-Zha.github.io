I am Kaidi Zha (查恺迪), a third-year undergraduate at Tsinghua University, where I study Mathematics and Physics. My research focuses on generative models and inference acceleration— making large diffusion and language models faster and cheaper to deploy through quantization, mixed-precision inference, and efficient system design, without sacrificing generation quality.

Research Interests Generative Models  ·  Inference Acceleration

Research Experience

Q-DiT: Accurate Post-Training Quantization for Diffusion Transformers
Research Assistant, supervised by Prof. Yuan Meng, Dept. of CS&Tech, Tsinghua University  ·  Jun. 2025 – Present

A timestep-aware, layer-wise mixed-precision PTQ framework for diffusion Transformers, driven by second-order sensitivity analysis (Hessian trace, GPTQ error bounds, Fisher information); near-lossless W4A4 across 7 DiT-family models—FID 7.76 on DiT-XL/2 256×256, and FID 25.22 on FLUX.2-klein-4B, surpassing the FP16 baseline (26.08).

ArchEGraph: A Large-Scale Graph Dataset for Geometry–Topology–Physics Aligned Building Energy Modeling
Research Assistant, supervised by Prof. Borong Lin, School of Architecture, Tsinghua University; with UC Berkeley & MIT  ·  Apr. 2026 – Present

Second author; designed and executed the benchmark suite for a building-energy graph dataset of 5,481 buildings and 49,326 validated simulation cases—evaluating 7 model families on topology reconstruction (M2G) and zone-level load prediction under cross-building and cross-climate shifts.

Selected Projects

TrainYourOwnGPT: Building & Analyzing GPT-Style Language Models from Scratch
Pattern Recognition & Machine Learning, course project (team of 2)  ·  Spring 2026
project

Trains GPT-style decoders from scratch (RoPE, SwiGLU, pre-norm) and studies scaling laws over 0.77M–5.46M params across 5 configs × 3 seeds—attention gating is the most reliable architectural gain; LoRA adaptation of Qwen2.5-0.5B eliminates exam-style outputs (4/15 → 0/15).

fair-SPLIT: Near-Optimal Decision Trees for Accuracy, Sparsity, and Fairness
Statistical Machine Learning, course project (team of 2)  ·  Spring 2026
project

Implements the SPLIT family—dynamic-programming lookahead trees that provably optimize the most discriminative top layers—and benchmarks it against CART on 12 datasets × 5 seeds; Leaf-Pareto Fair Recalibration cuts the demographic-parity gap from 0.17 to 0.003 on Adult.

AIDER: Taming Aleatoric Impulse in Off-Policy Reinforcement Learning
Reinforcement Learning & Control, course project (team of 8)  ·  Spring 2026
project

An EMA-anchored evidential regression operator (DEAR) that learns higher-order aleatoric uncertainty end-to-end, turned into schedule-free pessimistic targets and exploration bonuses—best average performance among 7 model-free baselines on the DeepMind Control Suite.

CPU Path Tracer: A Physically-Based Renderer in C++
Fundamentals of Computer Graphics, individual course project  ·  Spring 2026
project

A complete CPU renderer bridging Whitted ray tracing and Monte Carlo path tracing—next-event estimation, Cook–Torrance glossy BRDF, BVH, depth of field—with a 49× speedup on 64 threads and byte-identical deterministic output.
