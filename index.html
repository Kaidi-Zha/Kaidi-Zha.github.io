I am Kaidi Zha (查恺迪), a third-year undergraduate at Tsinghua University, where I study Mathematics and Physics. My research focuses on generative models and inference acceleration— making large diffusion and language models faster and cheaper to deploy through quantization, mixed-precision inference, and efficient system design, without sacrificing generation quality.

Research Interests Generative Models  ·  Inference Acceleration

Research Experience

Q-DiT: Accurate Post-Training Quantization for Diffusion Transformers
Research Assistant, supervised by Prof. Yuan Meng, Dept. of CS&Tech, Tsinghua University  ·  Jun. 2025 – Present
  • Co-designed a timestep-aware, layer-wise mixed-precision PTQ framework that allocates bit-widths (3/4/5-bit) per denoising timestep and per layer, driven by Hessian-trace, GPTQ-error and Fisher-information sensitivity metrics and solved by dynamic programming under a global W4A4 budget.
  • Refactored the codebase into qdiffusers, a modular YAML-driven quantization–evaluation pipeline adapted to 7 DiT-family models (DiT-XL/2, FLUX.2, PixArt-Σ, OpenSora, Qwen-Image, HunyuanVideo, Wan2.2) with multi-GPU parallel GPTQ support.
  • Led all ImageNet-1K experiments: W4A4 achieves near-lossless FID 7.76 on DiT-XL/2 256×256; on FLUX.2-klein-4B, W4A4 FID 25.22 even surpasses the FP16 baseline (26.08); OpenSora W4A8 drops VBench score by only 0.16%.
ArchEGraph: A Large-Scale Graph Dataset for Geometry–Topology–Physics Aligned Building Energy Modeling
Research Assistant, supervised by Prof. Borong Lin, School of Architecture, Tsinghua University; with UC Berkeley & MIT  ·  Apr. 2026 – Present
  • Second author of the manuscript and main contributor to the benchmark experiments of a dataset spanning 5,481 buildings and 49,326 validated simulation cases (>1.33×105 space nodes, 1.44×106 face nodes).
  • Designed and ran cross-building and cross-climate generalization benchmarks over 7 model families (XGBoost, DeepSets, Set Transformer, Perceiver, GATv2, TransformerConv, GraphGPS) on graph-reconstruction and load-prediction tasks.
