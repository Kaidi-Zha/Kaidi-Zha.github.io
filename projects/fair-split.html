fair-SPLIT: Near-Optimal Decision Trees for Accuracy, Sparsity, and Fairness

Statistical Machine Learning, course project (team of 2)  ·  Spring 2026

Code  ·  Report

Motivation

Greedy tree induction (CART) is myopic and offers no fairness guarantee—small lookahead errors at the top of the tree propagate downward, and accuracy-oriented splits can silently encode sensitive attributes.

Approach

Implemented the SPLIT family—dynamic-programming lookahead that provably optimizes the most discriminative top layers before greedy completion—and benchmarked it against CART on 12 public datasets (6 with sensitive attributes) × 5 seeds, measuring accuracy, training time, and SPD/DI/EOD metrics; SPLIT variants win on COMPAS, HELOC, Heart, and Law School.

Proposed Leaf-Pareto Fair Recalibration (LPFR), a deployable leaf-level post-processing method; label-aware calibration cuts the demographic-parity gap from 0.17 to 0.003 on Adult.
