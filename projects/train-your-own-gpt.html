TrainYourOwnGPT: Building & Analyzing GPT-Style Language Models from Scratch

Pattern Recognition & Machine Learning, course project (team of 2)  ·  Spring 2026

Code  ·  Report

Motivation

Understanding why language models scale—and when architectural tweaks and adaptation genuinely help—requires controlled experiments built from first principles, not off-the-shelf pipelines.

Contribution

Built the full stack from scratch—a GPT-style decoder with RoPE, SwiGLU, and pre-norm blocks, trained on Penn Treebank—and led the scaling-law and fine-tuning studies (team of 2): a 5-config sweep × 3 seeds, scaling over 0.77M–5.46M params, controlled ablations of QK-norm / attention gating / value embeddings, LoRA fine-tuning of Qwen2.5-0.5B (0.89% params) with pre-registered evaluation, and an AR vs. masked-diffusion LM (MDLM, LLaDA) comparison with A100 benchmarks.

Results

105.15 validation PPL for the best scratch model; attention gating is the most reliable architectural gain (−2.02 PPL); LoRA adaptation eliminates exam-style outputs (4/15 → 0/15) with 8× more abstract-style continuations.
